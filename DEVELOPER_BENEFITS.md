### 1. **Zero Context Switching**
*   **The Problem:** Alt-tabbing to a heavy Electron app (Slack/Teams) breaks your coding flow.
*   **The Solution:** Chat right where you code. Keep a terminal tab open and communicate without ever leaving your IDE environment.

### 2. **True "Air-Gapped" Communication**
*   **The Problem:** Corporate firewalls, restricted internet access, or working in a secure facility (SCIF).
*   **The Solution:** Use **LAN/Hotspot Mode**. No internet required. Chat completely offline over a local network or direct WiFi hotspot connection. Perfect for hackathons or secure office zones.

### 3. **Share Secrets Securely**
*   **The Problem:** You need to send an API key or a password to a teammate. Pasting it in Slack/Discord saves it on their servers forever.
*   **The Solution:** **End-to-End Encryption (AES-128-CBC)**. Your messages are encrypted *before* they leave your machine. Even the relay server sees only garbage text.

### 4. **Instant ephemeral rooms**
*   **The Problem:** Creating a Slack channel requires admin permissions and permanent clutter.
*   **The Solution:** Run `bugzx-chat`, type a room name `debug-session-404`, share the key, and you have an instant, private war room. When you close the terminal, it's gone.
